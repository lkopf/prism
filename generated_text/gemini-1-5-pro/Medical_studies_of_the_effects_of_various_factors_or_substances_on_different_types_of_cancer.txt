Medical studies exploring the link between ultraviolet (UV) radiation exposure and the development of skin cancer, including melanoma, basal cell carcinoma, and squamous cell carcinoma, have consistently demonstrated a strong positive correlation, with prolonged exposure to UV radiation from sunlight or tanning beds significantly increasing the risk of developing these malignancies, prompting public health campaigns advocating for sun safety practices such as seeking shade, wearing protective clothing, and using sunscreen with a high sun protection factor (SPF), while research efforts focus on understanding the molecular mechanisms by which UV radiation damages DNA and promotes skin cancer development, including the formation of pyrimidine dimers and oxidative stress, alongside investigations into the efficacy of novel therapies for treating skin cancer, including targeted therapies, immunotherapies, and photodynamic therapy, which utilizes light-sensitive drugs to selectively destroy cancer cells, offering hope for improved treatment outcomes and reduced morbidity and mortality associated with skin cancer.
